The Sacrifices We Make
24 Jun 2015
Akshat Baveja, ex-team leader and dedicated member writes on his four year long experiences with TeamKART and FSAE. He worked on the design and manufacture of K2, K2.2 and K3

To all those who are expecting a generic introduction to TeamKART or Formula SAE, Google us or find us on Facebook; I’m not here to introduce us.

Also, I don’t want to talk about how great this society/team/project is and how it will change your life, because that’s the case with every other student society. After a while these intros just become clichéd. I would rather talk about some things that I’ve learnt about this project that I’ve been a part of. Our team is very young and despite our very talented young members we have faced a rather difficult but a heck of an enjoyable time. Well simply put, there are four main aspects to making a Formula SAE car.

Money

You'll need a lot of it. As is the case with any custom built, hand crafted car. Where there is mass production of a commodity the cost of production becomes higher. Also, to those who are unfamiliar; this is not the average street car. It’s a high performance racing car (well, at least in its 600cc segment) and it needs the lightest parts possible without sacrificing load bearing capacity. Needless to say, such parts and materials are expensive.

Procurement

Here we are in a small town called Kharagpur. This place, which was planned to be an industrial hub is now so remote that even to watch a movie properly, one has to go to Kolkata which is a 3 hour sweaty and tiresome ordeal (and that’s one way). So imagine what it’s like to procure exotic parts for a car. Fast procurement and readily available parts are absolutely crucial to work on such a huge project. Really wish our team was based in Bombay.

Cooperative And Involved Administration

If the head of the snake is pointing in the wrong direction, there’s not much that the body can do. I dare not say more or I might get in trouble. See! That’s the problem. The lines of communication between the administration and the student needs to be open. However I don’t see a solution to this considering the administration has a lot of power over each individual student.

The Martyrs

Yes, I am referring to the team members who slog out day and night, summers and winters for a volunteering activity that not only sucks the life out of them but also sucks the money out of their small savings accounts. Yes these are the heavy hearted souls tensed about everything. And then there are those who dare to head this project. These are the pillars on which the whole team stands. Managing everything from marketing and finance to designing and manufacturing of the car to getting sponsors and convincing the professors to release another instalment of money and so on and so on. And that’s just the tip of the iceberg. Beneath the surface, a multitude of software packages to master, research and analysis to improve designs, managing unholy amounts of data and lastly and most importantly the gruelling hours of manual labour. Yes, we are proud to present to you a handcrafted car. Remember, this is a car created by students in their late teens and early twenties with minimal experience and only their inner flames to put in every ounce of energy to make such a car.

Well if need be I can digress more on the other aspects like team management, industrial support et cetera et cetera, but the important thing to understand here is that our team is still doing so well despite the many problems it continually faces. For those who don’t know, we have achieved second place in the business plan event as well as the cost report event while also clearing the 6 rigorous scrutiny tests at Formula Design Challenge 2015! Unfortunately, our piston rings were damaged beyond repair and we could not participate in the dynamic events. Nevertheless, this really calls for a big shout out to all the members who made this success possible. Over the past 4 years I’ve grown very fond of this project and will cherish my happiest and most painful experiences here forever. This is a project in a small corner of the world that I plan to support no matter where I am.
