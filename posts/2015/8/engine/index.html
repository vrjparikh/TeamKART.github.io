Engine
7 Aug 2015
An introduction to the engine team, for the freshers of 2015-16

The engine is the heart of a car.

An engine converts energy from the combustion of fuel into mechanical energy.

Most mainstream internal combustion engines today are 4-stroke engines. It replaces the older 2-stroke system. If you observe the animation of 4-stroke engine you will notice that fuel is burnt inside a given cylinder in every alternate piston stroke.

The 4 stroke cycle begins with Intake of fuel from the fuel injectors and air from the intake, followed by Compression of the air-fuel mixture. The mixture is then Burnt and then the exhaust gases are expelled in the next quarter phase. The four strokes are namely Intake, Compression, Combustion and Exhaust. The movement of the pistons turns the crankshaft whose rotation effectively turns the wheels.

Opening and closing of valves is controlled by a camshaft which is connected to the crankshaft via the timing belt which ensures that the entire system remains in sync.

Different kinds of engines follow different thermodynamic cycles. You may be familiar with the Carnot cycle.

A petrol engine uses the Otto cycle

A diesel engine uses the -wait for it- Diesel cycle.

See also: Atkinson cycle, Brayton cycle.

Optimum performance of the engine depends on many many factors such as how much air and fuel is available for combustion, how well exhaust gases are removed, timing of opening valves and ignition, lubrication, flow of fluids within, acoustics and even seemingly unimportant things like the shape and stiffness of the bolts and mounts that hold the engine in place. Furthur reading on engine tuning

It is the job of the Engine Subsystem to extract maximum power and torque from the engine while ensuring longevity and fuel economy by calculating the required parameters by solving complicated mathematical models using advanced software packages. The first step is finding the right engine which strikes the best balance between weight, performance and efficiency while staying within the boundaries of rules and regulations.

All of TeamKART cars upto 2015 used an Inline 4 cyl engine from the Honda CBR600RR. The 2015 car will be using a single cylinder engine from the KTM Duke390 for the first time ever.

This is followed by design and manufacture of accessory parts like the air intake, exhaust, engine oil sump, fuel tank, cooling and turbocharging systems.

A lot of cutting edge research and effort goes into these processes. Thus the Engine team becomes one of the most vital parts of any FSAE team.
