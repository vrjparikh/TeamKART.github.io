Corporate and Public Relations
6 Aug 2015
An introduction to the Corporate and Public Relations Team of TeamKART, for the freshers of 2015-16

Corporate and Public Relations plays a crucial role in any FSAE team.

Quite often, in an FSAE team, it may not be lack of technical expertise or talent pool that stops a team from building the perfect car.
It will be lack of funds. You need a lot of money to purchase parts and services. Car parts and premium materials don't usually come cheap.
If the team doesn't manage their finances properly and control income and expenditure they will run out of funds before the car is complete.

This regularly happens in professional racing as well. You may be aware of the F1 teams Caterham and Marussia who both went bankrupt during the 2014 season; which led to their immediate demise.

Income comes in the form of monetory and in-kind sponsorships. The team must maintain mutually beneficial relations with sponsors and toil to get more onboard.

Sponsors will get to know your team and functioning through the media. So the team should also ensure that they get media exposure and also have good presence on social media channels and the web. We also conduct workshops in other colleges.

This is where the CPR team comes in. Their prowess can make or break the team.

In addition to all these, CPR team is also responsible for the business logic event in FSAE which is worth 75 points. In the business logic event, the team is supposed to explain how they plan on producing and marketing the car for the consumer.

CPR team includes Webmasters and Designers, in addition for members for sponsorship and finance.

Selection Procedure

As the skill sets required for CPR is quite different from that of the Technical team, selection process is slightly different.

Written test for checking your analytical reasoning, followed by a group discussion testing communication skills and third, the final personal interview.

A minimum amount of technical knowledge about the parts and functioning of the car will be required in CPR work (placing orders, interacting with sponsors etc.)
So the CPR team is also expected to attend the workshops organised for the tech team trainees.
