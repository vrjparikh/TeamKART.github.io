What we do?
7 Aug 2015
An introduction to the various subsystems and functioning of TeamKART, for the freshers of 2015-16

TeamKART is the Formula SAE team of IIT Kharagpur.

Many of you may not be aware of Formula SAE. Formula SAE is a student design competition organized by SAE International. The competition was started in 1978 and is held every year in many countries all over the world; like the USA, the UK, Japan, Germany, Hungary, Brazil, Italy... Student teams from universities all over the world compete with each other. Cars may be Internal Combustion, Electric or Hybrid type.

FSAE is organised in a way that gives students the feel of Formula 1 and similar platforms. Just like F1, there is an elaborate, highly technical and strictly enforced set of rules and safety regulations (usually the most difficult part of the competition is getting approved in the Technical Inspection). FSAE teams function just like professional F1 teams. There are many famous engineers in leading F1 teams today who started out in FSAE.

But unlike F1, where the winner is the fastest car, the winner in FSAE is the best team; The best set of engineers. Judging is based not on speed or looks, but on design, market value, handling, cost efficiency, fuel efficiency and build quality. The goal is to create a really good car, not the fastest car.

Engineering. Not Speed

For students of IIT Kharagpur, TeamKART is the gateway to the world of FSAE.

"Formula Student success is not about building a good car, its about building a good team" -Claude Rouelle

Selection Procedure

Selection Procedure is long and rigorous.
The students have to first clear a test which based on logical thinking, mechanics, physics, aptitude and basic technical knowledge of the car. A series of workshops is organised for the qualified stuents in which they are taught about different subsystems of a car in detail. After these workshops, tasks and assignments are assigned for practice. This is followed by a final Personal Interview round. In this long process of selection, the candidates are continuously evaluated and finally the best candidates are selected to be the members of the team.
There are broadly six subsystems:

  • Engine
  • Transmission
  • Chassis and Aerodynamics
  • Suspension
  • Electronics
  • Corporate and public relations

All subsystems are equally important to the smooth functioning of the team and selection process is the same for all. You can join any subsystem of your choice based on interest.

Students are from different parts of the country, from different branches of engineering, but have a common passion. The members get the opportunity to interact and learn and explore the practical side of automobile engineering.

We will be introducing you to various subsystems in detail in the coming days.
